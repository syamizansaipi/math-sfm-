Optimizing Malaysian Oil Palm Yields Using Derivatives

Visualizing $f(x)$ and $f'(x)$ to pinpoint peak productivity ages.

🌿 The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysia, balancing land use and food security means maximizing Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) yields from oil palms (*Elaeis guineensis*). Palms have a distinct biological lifecycle: yield starts slow, surges exponentially during early maturity, peaks, and then gradually declines due to height-related harvesting difficulties and biological aging.

To maintain national food security without further deforestation, agronomists use derivatives to find the precise moment yield acceleration stops and when decline begins, helping plan perfect replanting cycles.

📐 Mathematical Modeling

Based on localized Malaysian yield curves (inspired by data from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Progress Reports), modeled as a cubic polynomial where $x$ is Palm Age (years) and $f(x)$ is Yield (tonnes/hectare/year):

Yield Function $f(x)$
f(x) = -0.008x³ + 0.28x² - 1.2x + 2
Rate of Change $f'(x)$
f'(x) = -0.024x² + 0.56x - 1.2
Calculated via the Power Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = n x^{n-1}$
